Learn about Dungeons and Dragons here!


This is the gist of Dungeons and Dragons:

If you've never played D&D, then it's definitely a lot to learn.
For example, you have to learn how to use all seven dice! ...although one of them is just the D10 but more.


For starters, D&D is a TableTop RolePlaying Game (TTRPG).
This means that you will be playing a game where you act out your character while playing over a game board.
This game board could be a full detailed map from a game module, or it could just be a rough sketch of some areas and things to interact with.
A game module is basically an official (or maybe even homebrewed) story that you can go on with your adventuring party.
*These modules usually can stand alone, and you could bring your same character through multiple modules, hence being called a "module".
Homebrew is essentially custom content. If there's something you want to do that doesn't exist already, ask your DM if you can try to homebrew it!
The Dungeon Master, or The Game Master, abbreviated as DM or GM, is the person at the table that acts as the world builder...
They're the ones hyping up the big boss coming up, describing a dark room with flickering candles, playing as the monsters and NPCs of the world, etc.


One of the best ways to learn how to play is just to ask other players in your games.
You can also learn on your own by looking stuff up on Google or Youtube, or you could use some of the resources on the "Get started" page.
